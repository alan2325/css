On a Nuwara Eliya, a town in Sri Lanka, is a popular tourist destination. It is known for its cool climate, stunning natural beauty, lush green tea plantations, cascading waterfalls, and beautiful gardens. Nuwara Eliya has a European-style environment, with colonial-style bungalows and a chilly climate. The British discovered the mountain town in the 19th century and turned it into a quaint town that mimicked life back in England. The town is still touted as ‘Little England’, an illusion maintained by the presence of the Nuwara Eliya Golf Club, a racecourse, the Victoria Park and excellent trout fishing in nearby lakes and rivers.

Paro is a valley town in Bhutan, located in the Paro Valley on the Paro River. It’s west of the capital, Thimphu, and is known for being the country’s only international airport. Paro is a historic town with many sacred sites and historical buildings. It was the country’s main cultural, commercial, and political center until 1962, when the capital was moved to Thimphu. Paro remains the summer capital. Paro also has some of the world’s most challenging biking trails. Other activities include hiking, trekking, rafting, kayaking, and stargazing. Paro is also home to many beautiful temples, monasteries, and villages. The town is also surrounded by stunning natural scenery, including the Paro River and the Himalayas. Paro is a great place to visit for tourists who are interested in learning about Bhutanese culture and history.
